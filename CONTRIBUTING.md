# Contributing - SOP Registry

This repository is the canonical recordkeeping venue for governance
instruments. See `DISCLAIMER.md` and `LICENSE`.

By submitting a Contribution (as defined in LICENSE Section 7), you agree to
the contribution terms in the LICENSE, including the grant-back to the DAO.
Repository Materials are Protocol IP.

## Who may contribute

- The **Head Steward** (Representative Agent) may adopt or amend SOPs on an
interim basis within existing authority, and may record interim instruments
here pending ratification.
- **Subcommittees** acting within their mandates may prepare drafts or inputs
for the Head Steward to record.
- **Members** may propose amendments or new instruments through the governance
process (via the proposal repository).

External contributions (pull requests from non-Members) are accepted subject
to the LICENSE contribution terms, but do not become governance instruments
unless adopted through the DAO's governance mechanisms and recorded through
the applicable Operational Pack process.

Acceptance, review, or merging of a contribution does not, by itself,
constitute legal advice, ratification, endorsement of any external
implementation, or direction/supervision of any Licensee's or
counterparty's operations.

## Role boundaries (no status or authority by repository role)

Repository contribution, review, maintainership, or moderation
is a repository administration function only. It does not, by itself:

- confer Member status, any Membership Interest, voting rights, or
economic rights;
- authorize any person to speak for or bind the DAO;
- create any agency, partnership, fiduciary, employment,
or supervisory relationship; or
- create authority to direct, supervise, or control the
operations of any Licensee, counterparty, or service provider.

Any governance authority, representation authority, or contractual
authority must arise from the Company Agreement, a valid Resolution,
or a separate written agreement.

## Recording instruments

### Ratified instruments

Instruments ratified through an Operational Pack proposal should be recorded
with:

1. The current ratified text.
2. A content identifier (hash or CID) matching the Operational Pack record.
3. The effective date.
4. A reference to the ratifying proposal (e.g. `DP-XXXXX`).

Where a repository instrument is intended to have effect under a
License or counterparty agreement, that effect must arise through
express incorporation by reference in the applicable written agreement.
Repository publication alone does not create obligations for
external parties.

### Interim instruments

Interim SOPs adopted by the Head Steward or subcommittees within existing
authority should be clearly marked as interim and must include:

1. The instrument text.
2. A content identifier.
3. The date of interim adoption.
4. A note that the instrument is pending ratification in the next
    Operational Pack.

Interim instruments:

-   bind only within the scope of existing authority;
-   do not create obligations for Members beyond existing obligations;
-   do not grant new rights to access or move Company Treasury Accounts; and
-   do not modify Primary or Secondary Instruments.

### Confidential entries

Confidential instruments are recorded by hash or content identifier only.
Do not commit cleartext confidential materials. See README Section 5.

## Update rules

-   **No in-place edits after ratification.** Amendments to ratified
    instruments require a new proposal (via the proposal repository) or a
    new interim adoption with a corresponding content identifier update.
-   **Superseded versions are retained** in git history for auditability.
-   **Secondary Instruments** (registries and registers) may receive
    record-keeping updates within the scope permitted by Operating Agreement
    Section 6.7, recorded here and submitted for ratification in the next
    Operational Pack.

## Document hierarchy

The Company Agreement comprises:

-   **Primary Instruments:** the Operating Agreement, the Founding Charter,
    and the IP License Framework.
-   **Secondary Instruments:** the Smart Contract Registry, Website
    Registry, Intellectual Property Registry, IP License & Assignment
    Register, and the Counterparty Agreements Register.
-   **Tertiary Instruments:** the SOP: Order of Business for Operational
    Packs, and this SOP Registry.

In the event of conflict, the order of precedence established in the
Operating Agreement applies.
Contributions that conflict with Primary or Secondary Instruments will
not be accepted.
