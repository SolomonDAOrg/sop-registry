## 1. What belongs here

This registry maintains the current ratified versions of:

- **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):** Company-side governance,
operational, and security procedures governing routine activities, controls,
and response processes for persons exercising authority under the Company
Agreement. SOPs are not, by themselves, operating manuals for Licensees
or counterparties except where a specific provision is expressly
incorporated into a separate written agreement.

- **Registries and Registers:** the Smart Contract Registry, Website
Registry, Intellectual Property Registry, IP License & Assignment Register,
Counterparty Agreements Register, and any other registries established
by Resolution.

- **Operational artefacts:** runbooks, key registries, security policies,
and similar Tertiary Instruments, to the extent they are published
(confidential instruments are recorded by hash or content identifier only).

- **Governance record packages:** Operational Pack ratification records,
change logs, and content identifiers.

## 2. What does not belong here

- **Proposal history and drafts.** These live in the proposal repository.

- **Confidential materials.** Private keys, signing infrastructure,
incident-response details, counterparty particulars, and other Confidential
Information or Security-Sensitive Information are never committed in
cleartext. Record only hash or content identifier references.

- **Government forms and identity materials.** Do not commit government
originals or KYC/identity artefacts. Record evidence anchors only.

- **Production Implementations.** Source code, binaries, deployment
configurations, trading logic, and operational methods are not governance
instruments.

## 3. Document hierarchy

The Company Agreement comprises:

- **Primary Instruments:** the Operating Agreement, the Founding Charter,
and the IP License Framework.

- **Secondary Instruments:** the Smart Contract Registry, Website
Registry, Intellectual Property Registry, IP License & Assignment Register,
and the Counterparty Agreements Register.

- **Tertiary Instruments:** the SOP: Order of Business for Operational
Packs, and this SOP Registry.

In the event of conflict, the order of precedence established in the
Operating Agreement applies. Tertiary Instruments may not expand authority
beyond what Primary and Secondary Instruments and on-chain constraints
permit.

## 4. Versioning and ratification

- Each instrument is maintained at its current ratified version.

- Superseded versions are retained for auditability (via git history and
annotated tags from the proposal repository).

- Interim SOPs adopted by the Head Steward or subcommittees within
existing authority are recorded here pending ratification through an
Operational Pack proposal.

- Ratified Operational Packs are recorded with their content identifiers
and effective date.

## 5. Confidential entries

The SOP Registry may include confidential entries recorded by:

1. a confidential hash or content identifier; and
2. metadata sufficient for integrity verification.

Confidential entries do not grant membership, voting rights, economic
rights, or authority beyond existing on-chain constraints. Full
particulars are disclosed only to a confidential reviewer or auditor under
appropriate controls, or in response to a lawful legal request.

Repository access, contribution history, maintainer status, or moderation
activity does not confer Member status, any Membership Interest, voting
rights, economic rights, or authority to bind the DAO.

## 6. Integrity verification

Each ratified instrument should include a content identifier (hash or CID)
recorded in the corresponding Operational Pack. Readers may verify
integrity by comparing the published content against the recorded
identifier.

Hash references to confidential instruments are integrity anchors only and
do not constitute publication of, or grant any license to, the underlying
material.
