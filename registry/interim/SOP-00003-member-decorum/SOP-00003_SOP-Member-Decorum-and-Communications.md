## 1. Purpose

1. This SOP establishes enforceable decorum and communications rules for
   Company-designated venues.
2. The intent is to keep those venues professional, high-signal, and safe
   so governance can run on facts, reproducible detail, and proposals.
3. This is a "lead by example" standard: a well-run DAO should be something
   others can point to and copy.
4. This SOP also reduces personal targeting of those administering
   governance venues by making moderation a DAO rule, not personal discretion.

---

## 2. Authority, hierarchy, and limits

### 2.1 Definitions

Terms defined in the Operating Agreement have the same meaning in this SOP
unless otherwise specified.

1. **Company-designated venue:** Any official governance venue designated
   in the Website Registry (Operating Agreement §2.1(4), §11.11), and any
   additional communications venue (repository discussions, official social
   accounts, etc.) that the Head Steward or a Subcommittee acting within
   delegated authority maintains or designates for Company purposes by
   Resolution or Operational Pack. Any additional Company-designated venue
   should be recorded in the Website Registry, SOP Registry, or applicable
   Operational Pack so the official status and scope of the venue is clear.

2. **Moderator:** A person or team authorized by the Head Steward or
   a Subcommittee acting within delegated authority to enforce venue rules.
   Moderator status is a delegated venue-enforcement role only.
   Moderator status, by itself, does not confer Member status, any
   Membership Interest, voting rights, economic rights, or authority
   to speak for or bind the DAO.

3. **Security-Sensitive Information:** Information whose disclosure
   increases exploit risk or materially compromises keys, incident response,
   custody, or proprietary operational methods (Operating Agreement §2.1(25)).

4. **Non-actionable accusation:** An allegation presented as fact without
   evidence, reproduction steps, specific references, or a path to
   verification, especially when repeated after being asked to provide
   actionable detail.

### 2.4 Scope of this SOP

This SOP governs:
 - participation conditions for Company-designated venues; and
 - a consistent moderation posture for applying those conditions.

Persistent bad-faith venue conduct may be documented and may be considered
as evidence relevant to good-faith and governance-integrity questions under
the Founding Charter and other Primary Instruments, to the extent permitted
by those instruments. This SOP does not itself create any new suspension,
expulsion, forfeiture, or economic penalty power.

This SOP governs venue access conditions and moderation posture only.
It does not adjudicate legal rights, contract rights, membership status,
membership rights, or license status, and it does not create authority
to direct, supervise, or control the operations of any Licensee,
counterparty, service provider, or other person.

## 3. Core principles

1. **Lead-by-example communications:** keep it factual, actionable, and high-signal.

2. **Criticism is allowed; harassment is not.**

3. **Behavioral standards apply equally:** no special rules for
   insiders, whales, or loud accounts.

4. **Good-faith channeling:** complaints must be moved toward actionable
   formats (facts, repro steps, governance proposals), not repeated
   disruption.

5. **Least drama, maximum clarity:** moderators focus on behavior and
   venue integrity, not ideology or personality.

6. **No entitlement posture:** the Head Steward, Subcommittees, Moderators,
   and other persons acting within delegated authority do not owe real-time
   engagement or support outside what Primary Instruments and applicable
   agreements require. If a Licensee or Licensee personnel participate in
   a Company-designated venue, this SOP applies to their venue conduct
   only and does not direct or supervise their independent operations.

## 4. Conduct rules in Company-designated venues

### 4.1 Prohibited conduct (non-exhaustive)

The following are prohibited in Company-designated venues:

1. Harassment, targeted abuse, dogpiling, discrimination, or sustained
   hostile tagging.

2. Threats (including threats of violence, extortion, or coercion).

3. Doxxing or sharing personal data without consent.

4. Spam, flooding, brigading, or coordinated disruption.

5. Impersonation (including pretending to be the Head Steward, a
   Subcommittee member, a moderator, or an official DAO representative).

6. Security endangerment, including requesting or posting
   Security-Sensitive Information (or exploit steps, key material,
   or operational secrets).

7. Deliberate fabrication, falsified evidence, or materially deceptive
   factual claims presented as verified fact in a manner reasonably likely
   to cause material harm to the Company, the integrity of Company-designated
   venues, the governance process, or personnel acting within delegated
   authority.

8. **Repeated non-actionable accusations presented as fact** after
   a moderator has:
   (a) redirected the user to provide actionable detail or the correct
       channel, and
   (b) issued a warning citing this SOP.
   After those two steps, repetition is treated as bad-faith disruption.

### 4.2 Allowed conduct (explicitly)

1. Critical opinions and sharp disagreement are allowed if they are
   not harassment, not threats, not doxxing, not impersonation, and
   not coordinated disruption.

2. Governance disputes are expected; venue disruption is not a
   substitute for governance process.

3. Good-faith whistleblowing is allowed where limited disclosure
   is reasonably necessary and does not create an avoidable security risk.

## 5. Representation and speaking for the DAO

1. No participant may present themselves as speaking for, binding, or
   representing the DAO unless explicitly authorized by Resolution and
   within that scope.

2. The Head Steward, Subcommittees, licensees, and their personnel act
   within defined scope; they are not the DAO.

3. Recommended public self-labeling:
 - "Member" / "community participant" (OK)
 - "Moderator" (OK if true)
 - "Official DAO spokesperson" (NOT OK unless designated)

4. Moderator status is a venue-enforcement role only and does not,
   by itself, authorize policy statements, legal statements,
   or representations on behalf of the DAO.

4. Moderator status alone does not make a person a Member and does
   not confer any Membership Interest or other Member rights under
   the Operating Agreement.

**Optional standard disclaimer (for volunteers/moderators):**  
> Not an official statement. Speaking in a personal capacity. Refer
to adopted proposals and registry items for canonical positions.

## 6. Moderation posture and enforcement

### 6.1 General standard

1. Moderation actions are taken to protect venue integrity and
   operational safety.

2. Moderation must be even-handed, proportionate, and focused on behavior.

3. Moderation actions under this SOP are venue-access and venue-integrity
   actions only. They are not legal determinations, do not bind the DAO
   outside the venue, and do not by themselves determine Member status,
   economic rights, or any contractual rights under a License or other
   agreement.

### 6.2 Progressive enforcement ladder (default)

Unless severity requires immediate escalation, moderation should apply:

1. **Redirect** (move to correct channel; request actionable format).

2. **Warning** (cite rule breached; request stop; state next step).

3. **Timeout / slowmode / limited posting** (cool-down).

4. **Temporary restriction** (channel read-only; role limitations).

5. **Temporary ban**

6. **Permanent ban**

### 6.3 Mandatory Class B escalation for repeated non-actionable accusations

1. If a user makes a non-actionable accusation presented as fact and a
moderator performs:
 - **one redirect**, then
 - **one warning**, and the user repeats the same non-actionable accusation
   (or substantially the same claim), then the conduct is treated as
   **Class B** and moderators should apply **at least** a timeout or temporary
   restriction, escalating to bans if repetition continues.

2. The goal is to stop coercive venue disruption while preserving the
   right to criticize and propose changes.

### 6.4 Immediate escalation (no ladder required)

Immediate mute/ban and evidence preservation where any of the following
occur:
- doxxing;
- credible threats;
- extortion/coercion;
- malicious impersonation;
- coordinated brigading;
- posting Security-Sensitive Information or exploit/keys.

### 6.5 Evidence and recordkeeping

1. Moderators should keep a minimal internal record: timestamp, handle,
   venue, rule, action, and a short quote/screenshot reference.
2. Public disclosure should be minimal ("user removed for doxxing/spam")
   unless more detail is necessary for governance integrity.

## 7. Appeals and review

1. A moderated user may request review once per action via the designated
   appeals path.
2. Appeals are reviewed for:
 - incorrect attribution (wrong person);
 - disproportionate action vs severity;
 - misunderstanding of the rule.
3. Governance may revise this SOP or override venue policy by Resolution
   / Operational Pack.

---

## 8. Routing complaints into governance

1. Operational complaints should be converted into one of:
 - a reproducible bug report / issue (facts + steps);
 - a governance proposal (policy change);
 - a formal complaint alleging breach of delegated authority (with evidence).
2. Repetitive venue disruption is not governance and may be treated as
   bad-faith coercion.

---

## 9. Relationship to Member duties and personnel protection

1. This SOP does not create support obligations or entertainment
   expectations.
2. Harassment/spam/brigading and repeated non-actionable accusations
   (after redirect + warning) are explicitly identified as venue misconduct
   and may be treated as bad-faith behavior under the Charter.
3. Moderation actions under this SOP are DAO policy implementation within
   delegated authority; targeting personnel personally for enforcing DAO
   policy is itself grounds for moderation.

## Appendix A -- Quick classification guide (non-binding)

- **Class A (Immediate ban):** doxxing, threats, extortion,
   Security-Sensitive disclosure, malicious impersonation.
- **Class B (Fast escalation):** coordinated brigading; repeated targeted
   harassment; spam flooding after warning; **repeated non-actionable
   accusations presented as fact after one redirect + one warning**.
- **Class C (Correctable):** off-topic posting; low-effort complaining;
   first-time non-actionable accusations (redirect -> warn -> Class B
   if repeated).
